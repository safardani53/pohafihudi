    Danehill Kodiac chasing prize money in the Longines Hong Kong Vase on Sunday

    DANEHILL KODIAC's owners are living the dream as they prepare to watch their gutsy performer run in the Longines Hong Kong Vase on Sunday. Richard Hannon's four-year-old will break from stall 10 at Sha Tin, but connections admit anything is a bonus after the journey he has taken them on.

    02 Aug 2024, 00:00

    DANEHILL KODIAC's owners are living the dream as they prepare to watch their gutsy performer run in the Longines Hong Kong Vase on Sunday.

    Richard Hannon's four-year-old will break from stall 10 at Sha Tin, but connections admit anything is a bonus after the journey he has taken them on.

    Part-owner Andy Smith is also expecting the quicker ground to suit.

    "This horse owes us nothing especially as we bought him with hurdling in mind, yet he's only gone and won a Group 3 at Ascot - beating a horse who was placed in the French Derby," said Smith.

    "When we've taken Robin Of Navan abroad, we've looked to local riders and it has tended to pay off, especially with the post positions to consider," she said.
