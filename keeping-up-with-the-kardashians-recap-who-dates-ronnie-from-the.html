    Last night on Keeping Up with the Kardashians Season 13 Episode 6, Kim rushed home after learning about what happened with ‘Ye.

    You don’t need to watch Keeping Up with the Kardashians online to know the situation we’re talking about, as it was ALL over the news.

    "I get a call from one of Kanye’s friends, and my heart drops. I am so scared, and I don’t know what to do," she said, recalling the horror.

    She never refers to the incident specifically, but in describing the phone call, there is no doubt she’s talking about Kanye’s epic meltdown.

    The chilling video above shows a portion of said call. 

    Meanwhile, Khloe Kardashian and her boyfriend, NBA star Tristan Thompson, stopped by Thanksgiving turkey drive in Cleveland, Ohio.

    Since she wasnt there, she later asked Scott Disick (of all people) how the family’s Thanksgiving celebration fared amid the ‘Ye situation.

    Scott replied, “There wasn’t a lot of people. You weren’t there. Kanye wasn’t there. Kim came, but obviously she wasn’t like, you know."

    "Pretty tired, down and out. I feel like everyone didn’t want to say certain things. It just felt a little bit uncomfortable,” The Lord added. 

    Kim eventually told Khloe: “I’m not in the mood to get into it right now. I’m just, like, emotionally drained and exhausted, but it will be OK."

    "Everything will be OK," she insisted. "This is forcing him to take much-needed time off … and I think that that will be just really good for him."

    Forced time off is what they’re calling a 5150.

    "I feel a sense of responsibility just with the pressure of taking care of everything … I think sometimes in life, you just need some time off.”

    Kim then implied that she hadn’t told her kids North and Saint anything about their dad, and that she’s just trying to act like all is well.

    Kardashian then revealed this sweet anecdote:

    "Kanye was really cute … he was like, ‘So this is what for better or for worse is.’ And I was like, ‘Yeah.’ … This is what being a wife is."

    Meanwhile, Khloe’s best friend Malika Haqq is dating her Famously Single costar, the former Jersey Shore cast member Ronnie Magro.

    The Keeping Up cast is loving/hating this.

    "How the f–k did this happen?" Khloe asked, while Scott later weighed in candidly: “This is ridiculous. They really think they’re in love?”

    “Let’s just see how long this lasts.”

    We’ll give it at lest three weeks.

    Kim’s take on it? Very Zen …

    “You can’t even feel bad," the struggling reality star told her sibling. "You’ve just gotta tell her, ‘Dude this is my one time. Let me live.’"

    Khloe then admitted feeling bad, adding, “This is so stupid in the grand scheme of life, and you’re obviously dealing with real s–t.”

    Malika then proved Scott right by revealing that the romance took a “huge turn for the worse,” and Khloe reverted to protective mode:

    “I’m never gonna not be Malika’s friend … and maybe she’s just embarrassed that she’s dating Ronnie from the Jersey Shore."

    "Because I would be.”

    Damn. That’s harsh, but sometimes it’s the truest words that cut the deepest, and let’s face it, he is Ronnie from (the) Jersey Shore.

    Finally, Kendall and Kylie Jenner took center stage as the duo’s fledgling fashion line was reviewed by Nicole Phelps of Vogue magazine.

    Kendall admitted she was nervous, too.

    “I’ve worked really hard to be taken seriously and recognized in Vogue and the fashion industry. Everyone wants an excuse to talk s–t.

    "And I don’t want this to be that.”  

    The review was pretty vanilla to start, causing Kendall to remark that “I’m gonna puke,” but it turned out to be better than thought.

    Vogue loved every piece in the collection, to which all we can say is congrats to Kendall for making her fashion dreams a reality.

    And to Kylie, for being Kylie.
