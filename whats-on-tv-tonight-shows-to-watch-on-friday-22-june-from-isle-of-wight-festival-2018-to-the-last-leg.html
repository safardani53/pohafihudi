    WHAT can't you miss on TV tonight? Our guide to the must-see shows...

    Isle of Wight Festival 2018, 7pm, Sky Arts

    Glastonbury is taking a well-earned year off, which is good news for cows, but what about live music fans?

    Rival music festivals are seizing summer 2018 and running with it. Presenters Edith Bowman, Vick Hope and Joel Dommett are on hand to help you enjoy a stellar line-up at Seaclose Park, Newport, where the likes of The Killers, Depeche Mode, Manic Street Preachers, Jessie J, Camila Cabello and Liam Gallagher perform over this three-day weekend.

    Hitting the stage tonight, though, are Kasabian, Nile Rodgers & Chic and Rita Ora (right).

    The Last Leg, 10pm, Channel 4

    Way before brilliant Britain’s Got Talent winner Lost Voice Guy made poking fun at his own disability cool, Adam Hills (centre) and Alex Brooker (right) were doing it on this show.

    Now back for a 14th series, they’re joined once again by comic Josh Widdicombe (left) to take a swipe at the biggest stories of the week.

    Cruising with Jane Mcdonald, 9pm, Channel 5

    This week and next, Jane (above) discovers the joys of South America.

    In Punta Arenas, Chile, she is hand-fed local lamb, washed down with Pisco Sour cocktails.

    From there, she travels to the Falklands and chats with war veterans before heading to Argentina, where she attempts to tango.

    It’s clearly not a bad gig, this cruising lark.

    Dale Winton’s Florida Fly Drive, 10pm, Channel 5

    Dale Winton's final TV appearance on Florida Fly Drive

    It’s nice to see that TV presenter Dale (below) spent a glorious month in his favourite place shortly before his death.

    During the third week of his trip to Florida, a quiz night at a British-themed pub in Naples sets him off, reminiscing about his first booking as quizmaster at the Old King Lud on London’s Fleet Street in 1976. Before you know it, he’s hosting the evening!

    The Graham Norton Show, 10.45pm, BBC One

    If we could turn back time, we wouldn’t believe how successful the movie Mamma Mia! would be.

    So much so that Cher (above) is starring in the prequel. Named Mamma Mia!

    Here We Go Again, Cher plays Donna’s (originally played by Meryl Streep) mum.

    Get all the goss from her tonight.
