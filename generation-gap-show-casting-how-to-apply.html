    GENERATION Gap is a family-centered game show that has piqued the interest of many TV viewers.

    Now, fans want to know how to be cast on the popular television series.

    What is Generation Gap?

    Generation Gap is a comedy quiz game show that airs on ABC and is hosted by actress Kelly Ripa.

    It debuted on July 7, 2022, and is now in its second season.

    ABC's description of the TV series states that it "pairs teams of seniors and juniors, challenging them to answer questions about pop culture from each other's generations."

    It is produced by MGM Television, Kimmelot, and Milojo.

    Read More On Generation Gap

    Ripa also serves as an executive producer alongside her husband, Mark Consuelos.

    Other executive producers include Mark Burnett, Jimmy Kimmel, Albert Bianchini, Alycia Rossiter, Jonathan Kimmel, and Barry Poznick.

    How does casting work for Generation Gap?

    Seniors can fill out an online application form for the chance to be selected as a Generation Gap contestant, while a parent or legal guardian must apply on behalf of juniors.

    The show features children between the ages of eight and 15 and senior adults who are about 65 or older.

    When filling out the form, applicants should first choose a teammate.

    As the senior application form states, teammates should be each other's close friend or family member.

    After submitting the form, Generation Gap's casting team will "soon" review the application and may call the applicant.

    It is also possible to nominate someone for the show by emailing gengapcasting@amazon.com.

    When do new episodes of Generation Gap air?

    New episodes of Generation Gap air Thursdays at 8pm ET, 7pm CT on ABC.

    Fans can also stream the show via Hulu.

    Season two premiered on June 29, 2023, with the second episode airing the following week on July 6.

    The third episode of the season premiered on July 13, and the fourth is set to air on July 20.
