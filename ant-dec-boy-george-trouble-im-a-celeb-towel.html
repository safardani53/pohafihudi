    Ant and Dec accuse Boy George of causing trouble in Im A Celeb camp

    I'M A Celebrity... Get Me Out Of Here hosts Ant and Dec have accused Boy George of being behind the missing towels in camp. The TV presenters headed to their Instagram page to speculate on which of the celebs had been causing trouble in camp.

    31 Aug 2024, 00:00

    I'M A Celebrity... Get Me Out Of Here hosts Ant and Dec have accused Boy George of being behind the missing towels in camp.

    The TV presenters headed to their Instagram page to speculate on which of the celebs had been causing trouble in camp.

    Last night, DJ Chris Moyles was seen fuming around the jungle camp as he revealed his towel had been taken for the third day in a row.

    The former Radio One star took to the Bush Telegraph to accuse his co-star Boy George, 61, of being behind the missing item.

    Now, Ant, 46, and Dec, 47, have weighed in on the debate in a hilarious social media video after announcing the latest trial.

    "Do we need to spend much time of towel-gate? I think we all know who has been stealing the towels," Dec said with a smile.

    "Yeah we do, we absolutely do - it's Boy George! We can see him!" Ant added, as he explained they had watched him move a towel.

    "We saw him take a towel and take it off and put it on his shoulder. It's got to be Boy George."

    It comes after Chris, 48, was left raging that his towel had gone missing "three days in a row" as chaos broke out in camp.

    Once away from his camp mates, Chris then accused 80s pop star Boy George of repeatedly moving his towe from its place.

    He said: "This is killing me, I like to be as clean as possible. And I’m dirty. I don’t like it. I think I know who it is. George.” 

    Chris' temper had flared as he walked around the I'm A Celebrity camp in Australia looking for his own towel.

    He complained to the celebrities: “Guys, with the risk of sounding like a germaphobe with cleanliness issues, is there any chance that when I dry my towel and I go to get it the next day that it’s still there?

    "Three days in a row and I go to get it and it’s not there.

    "As much as I really, really like you all, I don’t want to dry my body with the stench of your balls.” 

    Unaware that Chris was mentally holding him responsible, George helpfully suggested: “They should have embossed the towels with our names.” 

    Fans found themselves chuckling at the tense scenes, with many sharing their thoughts on Twitter.

    One tweeted: "After days of Boy George 'meditating' Chris Moyles has realised what he's been chanting 'TOOK YOUR TOWEL'."

    Another added: "And now we have towel-gate," as a third concluded: "Why would you wanna use his towel? Gross!"

    Camp leader Matt Hancock discussed the rising tensions over Chris' missing towel in the Bush Telegraph.

    The MP said: “Being camp leader, it’s about when there’s a problem, just trying to find a solution that everybody’s happy with. This is a happy camp and I want to keep it that way.”
