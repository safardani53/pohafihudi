    Inventing Anna: Who is in the cast and how can I watch?

    ANNA Sorokin is a convicted con artist who pretended to be a German heiress with the last name Delvey from 2013 until 2017, using her platform to defraud banks, hotels, and wealthy acquaintances. On Friday, February 11, 2022, the series Inventing Anna is set to hit screens.

    01 Jun 2024, 00:00

    ANNA Sorokin is a convicted con artist who pretended to be a German heiress with the last name Delvey from 2013 until 2017, using her platform to defraud banks, hotels, and wealthy acquaintances.

    On Friday, February 11, 2022, the series Inventing Anna is set to hit screens.

    What is Inventing Anna?

    Inventing Anna is a Netflix drama mini-series created and produced by Shonda Rhimes.

    The show is based on a 2018 The Cut article, 'How Anna (Sorokin) Delvey Tricked New York's Party People' written by Jessica Pressler.

    On Netflix, Inventing Anna's plot synopsis reads: "Audacious entrepreneur or con artist? A journalist chases down the story of Anna Delvey, who convinced New York's elite she was a German heiress."

    Who is in the cast?

    Julia Garner as Anna Delvey

    Born on February 1, 1994, Julia Garner is an up-and-coming actress known for her roles on shows such as Ozark and The Americans.

    Garner has won two Primetime Emmy Awards for her work on Ozark and has also appeared in the Netflix miniseries Maniac.

    From 2018 until 2019, Garner starred in the Bravo true-crime series, Dirty John.

    Most read in TV

    Anna Chlumsky as Vivian

    Born on December 3, 1980, Anna Chlumsky is an actress known best for her lead role in the 1991 film, My Girl.

    After appearing in the 1994 sequel, Chlumsky went on to star in Blood Car, In the Loop, and Veep.

    While working on Veep, Chlumsky received six Primetime Emmy Award nominations.

    Katie Lowes as Rachel

    Born on September 22, 1982, Katie Lowes is an actress and theater director.

    She rose to fame during her time as Quinn Perkins on the political drama series Scandal.

    Laverne Cox as Kacy Duke

    Born on May 29, 1972, Laverne Cox is an actress and LGBTQ+ advocate.

    She first rose to prominence for her role on Orange Is the New Black, for which she became the first transgender person to be nominated for a Primetime Emmy Award since 1990.

    Cox won a Daytime Emmy Award for her work on Laverne Cox Presents: The T Word.

    Alexis Floyd as Neff

    Alexis Floyd is an actress and producer, who has worked on The Bold Type and Newly Single.

    Arian Moayed as Todd

    Born on April 15, 1980, Arian Moayed is an Iranian-born actor, writer, and director.

    He received a Tony Award nomination for his performance in Bengal Tiger at the Baghdad Zoo.

    Moayed also appeared in the Tony-winning show, The Humans.

    He is known as Stewy Hosseini on HBO's Succession.

    Anders Holm as Jack

    Born on May 29, 1981, Anders Holm is an actor, comedian, writer, and producer.

    He created and starred in the Comedy Central sitcom Workaholics, and also appeared in the series Champions.

    Holm is a co-founder of the sketch comedy group, Mail Order Comedy.

    Anna Deavere Smith as Maud

    Born on September 18, 1950, Anna Deavere is an actress, playwright, and professor.

    She worked on The West Wing from 2000 until 2006, and also starred on Nurse Jackie and For the People.

    Jeff Perry as Lou

    Born on August 16, 1955, Jeff Perry is known for his work on and off the stage.

    He portrayed Richard Katimski on My So-Called Life and Thatcher Grey on Grey's Anatomy.

    Perry has also appeared on Scandal and Nash Bridges.

    Terry Kinney as Barry

    Born on January 29, 1954, Terry Kinney is an actor and theater director.

    He is a founding member of the Steppenwolf Theatre Company, alongside John Malkovich, Laurie Metcalf, Gary Sinise, and Jeff Perry.

    Jennifer Esposito as Talia Mallay

    Jennifer Esposito is an actress and author.

    She has starred in multiple films, including Crash, Summer of Sam, Don't Say a Word, Taxi, and Welcome to Collinwood.

    Esposito has also appeared across television, starring in shows like Blue Bloods, Mistresses, and NCIS.

    How can I watch Inventing Anna?

    Inventing Anna will be available to watch on Netflix starting on Friday, February 11, 2022.

    The mini-series will feature nine one-hour-long episodes.
