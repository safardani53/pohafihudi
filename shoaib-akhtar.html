    Career Succession & Professional Career of The World’s Fastest Bowler

    Shoaib Akhtar’s Debut In The International Cricket

    Regarded as one of the fastest bowlers, his international debut came against the West Indies. Meanwhile, when West Indies came to play with Pakistan in 1997/1998.

    Rise To Fame

    In just a few years since his debut, he showed high-class bowling against India in 1999. Furthermore, Shoaib played well against Australia in 2002.

    Besides playing with Australia, he had a significant game at Asian Test Championship. Apart from this, the Pakistani player bagged success in the 1999 Cricket World Cup.

    In 2005, this magnificent player performed exceptionally well in a three-test match home series against England.

    His slower deliveries and striking pace were unplayable by the English batters; he was not only good, but he also took 17 total wickets in the series.

    Poor Performance of Shoaib Akhtar

    There is no doubt that he has got both strength and speed. However, ups and downs come in everybody’s life.

    Career Stats, Wickets, And Records

    Talking about his records, Akhtar took 178 wickets with a bowling average of 25.69 in test matches. In ODI games, Shoaib took 247 wickets with a bowling average of 24.97. 

    In the T20 format, he played 15 matches and bowled 318 balls while taking 19 wickets with an economy rate of 8.15.

    While playing with Pakistan, he won several awards, including “Man of the Match” and “Man of the Tournament.”

    Club Career & Retirement

    The Sultan of Cricket has played for three English cricket clubs, Somerset in 2001, Durham in 2003, and Worcestershire in 2004 & 2005.

    While playing with Durham, Akhtar had the maximum number of wickets with fabulous performances. 

    Apart from this, Akhtar made his IPL debut on May 13, 2008, playing with the Kolkata Knight Riders. In his debut match, Akhtar took four wickets against the Delhi Daredevils.

    In IPL, he played 3 matches, bowled 7 overs, and took 5 wickets with an economy rate of 7.71.

    The Pakistani-born retired from International Cricket after the 2011 Cricket World Cup. His last match was against New Zealand, where the Kiwis won by 110 runs.

    Speed Star Akhtar | Cricket Controversies, Injuries & Drug Scandal

    The mercurial and fearsome fastest bowler has also been part of controversies during his professional career.

    After Pakistan’s pathetic performance in the 2003 World cup, he had a verbal conflict with the former Pakistani captain and fast bowler Waqar Younis. 

    In the 2003 series against Sri Lanka, he was banned for two one-day internationals for ball-tampering and was fined 75% of his match fee. The same year, Shoaib was again banned for some matches for abusing South-African player Paul Adams.

    In August 2007, he used some disgusting words against Pakistan Cricket Board (PCB). As a result, PCB imposed a fine of $3.5k for his indiscipline and bad behavior in a cricket camp in Karachi.

    Besides, he was also banned for five years in 2008 for violating the code of conduct. Furthermore, in the 2009 ICC World Cup T20, he was dropped from the Team for genital viral warts. 

    The ICC again suspended him over ball tampering charges as he was caught on camera meddling with the ball in an ODI match against England in 2010. To which, he claimed that tampering was standard practice.

    Injuries

    In 2004, he suffered a back and wrist injury while playing against India. Again in 2005, he suffered from a hamstrings problem and was sent back to Pakistan.

    Apart from this, he also underwent knee and ankle injuries, which forced him to undergo surgery in Feb 2006.  

    Drug Scandal

    Akhtar and Mohammad Asif were both suspended in 2006 by PCB. The reason being they both tested positive for using nandrolone.

    Later in 2007, the case was dropped, and they both were allowed to play.
