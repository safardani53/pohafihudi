    JEOPARDY! player Ray LaLonde has annoyed viewers with his on-stage habit before clinching another victory.

    Ray from Toronto, Canada was on a nine-day winning streak at the start of the December 28 Jeopardy! episode.

    The champ started the episode with a whopping $255,100 in the bank.

    Before the first break, Ray had already won thousands of dollars, while his competitors only had $600 and $800 on the board.

    By the end of round one, Ray won over $10,000, and by the end of Double Jeopardy, his total stood at $23,400.

    The category for Final Jeopardy was America at War, and the question was: "Until the Civil War, the January 8 date of this battle of dubious military importance but big morale value was a national holiday."

    Ray had the correct answer of "The Battle of New Orleans," and he risked $6,600.

    He left this episode as a 10-day champ and an additional $30thousand to add to his total.

    MOVE IT OR LOSE IT

    Throughout the entire episode, Ray could be seen twisting his body repeatedly from left to right, grabbing the attention of viewers.

    "Stop that swaying, please," one person wrote while another touched on Ray's "annoying habit."

    TEN EPISODES OF SWAYING

    During a previous episode, where he scored his ninth win, Ray took some extra time during the chit-chat portion to send a sweet message.

    He gave a special thank you and shout-out to the crew working on the show.

    “Speaking of gratitude, I wanted to say something while I’m up here," Ray shared.

    He continued: “To the contestant crew here who’ve been the nicest, most patient, kindest people in the world, and just made this experience a joy.”

    The studio audience ate him up and gave him a loud round of applause.

    Host Ken Jennings replied: “That’s fantastic. We want everyone to have a good time on Jeopardy!. I’m glad it’s working."
