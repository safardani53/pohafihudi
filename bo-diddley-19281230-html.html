    Ellas McDaniel, known as Bo Diddley, is an American singer, guitarist, musician and music producer who played a key role in the transition from blues to rock and roll. He has influenced many artists, including Buddy Holly, Elvis Presley, The Beatles, The Rolling Stones, Animals and Clash. The use of African rhythms and its characteristic rhythm, a simple five-tone hambone rhythm, is the basis of hip hop, rock and pop music. In recognition of his accomplishments, he was honored at the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1987, the Blues Hall of Fame in 2003, and the Rhythm and Blues Music Hall of Fame in 2017. He has received a Lifetime Achievement Award from the Rhythm and Blues Foundation and a Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award. Diddley is also recognized for his technical innovations, including his distinctive rectangular lute, with its unique, resonant, and sparkling sound.
