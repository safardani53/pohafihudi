    This included a package of assorted cupcakes, a large carrot cake for $7.99 and several varieties of cookies.

    SAVING AT ALDI

    Aldi is already a relatively cheap grocery store due to the way it’s operated: it relies on fewer employees and a smaller selection of products to keep prices low.

    But there are ways you can save even more next time you shop at the chain.

    First, make sure you always shop the Aldi brands for the best deals.

    These include Specially Selected and Simply Nature, which both tend to be better for your health and more cost-friendly.

    The brand offers tons of products that the name brands do but for much cheaper - up to 50 percent less.

    Of course, as mentioned, another way to save at the store is to check the Aldi Finds aisle.

    The section is similar to the clearance section in many stores.

    The grocery chain has rotating merchandise that includes limited-time, specialty products.

    You should also time your shopping trip strategically at Aldi.

    Wednesday is the best day to shop as Aldi Finds go live on this day.

    It’s also a good idea to go in with a plan or a list in order to stay on track and avoid impulse buys.

    You can also save more money by using cashback apps that offer rewards, as they can help with toys as well as groceries.

    Ibotta is one of the most popular ones to use, with the company claiming its users save an average of $150 per year.
