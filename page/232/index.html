07 Apr 2024, 00:00

Ida Lupino was an English-American actress, singer, director, and producer. She is widely regarded as the most prominent female filmmaker working in the 1950s during the Hollywood studio system. With her independent production company, she co-wrote and co-produced several social-message films and became the first woman to direct a film noir with The Hitch-Hiker in 1953. Among her other directed films the best known are Not Wanted about unwed pregnancy, Never Fear loosely based upon her own experiences battling paralyzing polio, Outrage one of the first films about rape, The Bigamist and The Trouble with Angels.
