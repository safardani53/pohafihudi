    18 Apr 2024, 00:00

    THIS is the shocking moment two grown men brawled with dozens of school kids and launched kung fu-kicks before getting beaten on the floor.

    Cops have launched an investigation after pupils at de Ferrers Academy in Burton-on-Trent, Staffordshire, were filmed fighting the two masked adults.

    Startling footage posted online shows a group of uniformed children laughing on a pavement before one makes a hand gesture towards one of the men.

    The man steps forward, prompting some of the children to run into the drive of a nearby house.

    Screams can be heard as the two men launch kung fu kicks at the children in the ensuing mass brawl.

    One of the men is beaten to the ground, with the other toppling along with a child onto the bonnet of a parked car.

    Pupils then circle the car before both adults are beaten on the floor, with one kicked in the head by a youth.

    Both men then get up and continue fighting, with the confrontation ending in a tense stalemate.

    The owners of the house and the car were not involved in the brawl.

    Staffordshire Live reports that parents at de Ferrers Academy were told: "You may be aware of and have seen social media coverage of an incident that occurred on Friday, March 24 at approximately 8.30am prior to the school day commencing.

    "This incident took place away from the school premises.

    "We are currently working in conjunction with Staffordshire Police and will follow all guidance and direction given to us as this is an ongoing police investigation.

    "We will continue to follow our own internal policies where necessary.

     "We are also aware of malicious rumours surrounding another event at the academy.

    "I would kindly ask that you do not speculate and post inaccurate information on social media platforms, as this is unhelpful.

    "We will of course communicate with parents in due course with any updates regarding the above, should the need arise."

    Staffordshire Police said: "We were called at 8.45am on Friday (24 March) to reports of disorder outside De Ferrers High School in Burton.

    "Enquiries into the circumstances are ongoing and patrols have been increased in the area.
