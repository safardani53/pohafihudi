    Family's heartbreak as man, 20, found murdered in woodland

    02 Jun 2024, 00:00

    THE family of a young man found murdered in woods in Cambridgeshire have said they have "no forgiveness" for his killer.

    Sam Mechelewski, from Huntingdon, Cambs, was found dead by a member of the public in a wooded area of Hinchingbrooke Country Park at 1.40pm on Thursday.

    Police believe he was the victim of a "targeted attack" and are appealing for witnesses.

    In an emotional statement, the 20-year-old's family urged their son's killer to turn themselves in.

    It read: "The last 24 hours have been the worst of our lives. Sam will be greatly missed by his family and friends.

    "Sam was no angel, but his life should not have ended this way. If anyone has information please contact the police."

    It added: "We have no forgiveness for the person who has done this, but you are also someone's child.

    "I urge you to come forward and take responsibility for your actions."

    A post-mortem was took place last night but Cambridgeshire police said no further details were available at this stage.

    Officers are conducting searches close to the scene and making house-to-house enquiries in the area.

    MOST READ IN NEWS

    Det Ch Insp Adam Gallop said: "We believe this was a targeted attack that has sadly resulted in the death of a young man.

    "We're slowly piecing together Sam's movements from 8pm onwards on Wednesday January 31.

    "I appeal to anyone who knows Sam and saw him that evening, or anyone who was in the park and noticed anything suspicious, to contact us as a matter of urgency."
