    Daytime TV’s best and brightest descended upon Downtown Los Angeles on Friday for the 50th annual Daytime Emmy Awards.

    Susan Lucci was honored with the Lifetime Achievement Award, a distinction presented by Shemar Moore, who also presented Lucci with her first Daytime Emmy in 1999 after 19 nominations for her portrayal of Erica Kane on All My Children.

    Meanwhile, The Kelly Clarkson Show, General Hospital and The Bold and the Beautiful were big winners with several statuettes each.

    Sonya Eddy, who died on Dec. 19, 2022, at the age of 55, won a posthumous award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Daytime Drama Series for her performance on General Hospital.
