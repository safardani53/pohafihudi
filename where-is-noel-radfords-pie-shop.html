    THE Radfords are known for being Britain's largest family.

    However, they also have their own pie business, but where is it?

    Where is the Radford family pie shop?

    Noel Radford's pie shop is located in Heysham, Lancashire, near their family home.

    Britain's biggest family have owned the bakery since 1999.

    What is it called?

    Noel's pie shop is named after his family.

    Simply called Radford's Pie Company, there is no mistake about who is in charge of the day-to-day runnings of the business.

    Read more on The Radfords

    How do the Radford's make their money?

    Noel has admitted that the money they make through the bakery goes towards any family costs.

    On their website, Noel says: "Hello, I'm Noel! Some of you might know me and my family from our TV programme 15, 16, 17, 18, 19, 20, 21, and 22 Kids And Counting from Channel 4 and Channel 5.

    "You guessed it, we are Britain's biggest family!

    "We have owned our own lovely bakery since 1999 which is how we manage to provide for (and feed) our huge and expanding family as well as for the local people of Heysham and Morecambe."

    Can Radford pies be cooked from frozen?

    Detailed instructions of how to cook the pies are available online.

    The instructions say: "You can cook the family pies in just 25 mins at 180°C / 160°C fan / 350°F gas mark 4 or the standard pies for 20 minutes."

    The website warns that pies need to be defrosted before reheating.

    How much do Radford pies cost?

    The company serves a wide selection of pies, from a meaty British steak and stilton, or tender chicken and gammon to totally vegetarian ones.

    Pies start from £3.75.

    You can also get a pie pack which contains 10 different pies starting at £35.50.

    Who works for The Radford's Pie Company?

    Due to being a family business, you can expect the Radfords themselves to be involved.

    Noel has been a baker for 25 years, working in a variety of bakeries until he opened his own.

    His wife Sue is also involved in the business.

    When she isn't looking after the children, Sue looks after any orders they get as well as acting as a taste tester.

    Meanwhile, two of the couple's 22 children also work for the family business.

    Chloe has helped make pies for the last five years as well as suggesting new recipes, according to Noel.

    Daniel also works at the bakery, taking orders, preparing pies for delivery and making sure everything is spotless.
